Dr. Jose Rizal

"A man who died a hero for the future!"

"National hero, visionary, and advocate for freedom - Jose Rizal's legacy continues to inspire."

  • June 19, 1861: Jose Rizal is born in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines.
  • 1887: Rizal completes his novel "Noli Me Tangere," a work that exposes the social ills in the Philippines.
  • He publishes his second novel, "El Filibusterismo," a sequel to "Noli Me Tangere."
  • 1889: Rizal establishes the La Liga Filipina, a civic organization advocating for reforms.
  • 1892: Rizal is arrested and exiled to Dapitan in Mindanao.
  • 1896: The Katipunan, a revolutionary society, launches a revolt against Spanish rule; Rizal is implicated.
  • December 30, 1896: Jose Rizal is executed by firing squad in Bagumbayan, now Luneta Park, Manila.
  • 1897: The "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" are banned by Spanish authorities.
  • 1901: The American colonial government lifts the ban on Rizal's novels.
  • 1912: The Rizal Monument in Luneta Park is inaugurated in honor of Jose Rizal.
  • 1930: Rizal is officially declared a national hero by the Philippine government.
  • 1942-1945: The Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II impacts the commemoration of Rizal's life and works.
  • Rizal is canonized as a saint by the Philippine Independent Church.
  • 1996: The National Historical Institute installs a marker at the site of Rizal's execution, renaming it Rizal Park.
  • 2021: The Philippines commemorates the 160th birth anniversary of Jose Rizal with various events and activities.
