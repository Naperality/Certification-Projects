Roman Numeral Converter

Roman Numeral - this numeric system were commonly used in the past for various purposes, such as numbering the chapters of books, denoting monarchs and popes, and in clock faces. While they are not as widely used in everyday arithmetic today, they still have cultural and historical significance, and you may encounter them in specific contexts like the numbering of movie sequels, outlining parts of documents, or in the names of monarchs (e.g., Queen Elizabeth II).

  • V have a value of "5".
  • X have a value of "10".
  • L have a value of "50".
  • C have a value of "100".
  • D have a value of "500".
  • M have a value of "1000".
